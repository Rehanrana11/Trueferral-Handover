CORE QUESTION (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

What do people do today when introductions happen but outcomes disappear?
Why do those workarounds fail repeatedly?

⸻

THE REALITY WE’RE MAPPING (PROBLEM IN ONE LINE)

Introductions happen across email + LinkedIn + WhatsApp + SMS + Slack — but outcome status is invisible, so the introducer loses closure, reputation confidence, and leverage.

⸻

✅ 7 WORKAROUNDS PEOPLE USE TODAY (AND WHY THEY FAIL)

1) “Awkward Follow-Up Ping”

How it works:
Introducer sends: “Hey just checking if you two connected?”

Why it fails:
	•	Social friction (feels needy/annoying)
	•	Often ignored or delayed
	•	Creates reputational discomfort

Cost:
	•	5–15 minutes per intro (mental + message)
	•	Stress + hesitation to intro again

⸻

2) Double-Chasing Both Parties

How it works:
Introducer separately messages both people:
	•	“Did you reply?”
	•	“Did you schedule?”

Why it fails:
	•	Introducer becomes a project manager
	•	Creates pressure / annoyance
	•	People ghost to avoid replying

Cost:
	•	10–25 minutes per intro
	•	High emotional tax

⸻

3) Trusting “They’ll Handle It”

How it works:
Introducer does nothing and assumes it worked.

Why it fails:
	•	No closure signal
	•	Leads to uncertainty loops (“Did I waste that intro?”)
	•	Next intro request feels risky

Cost:
	•	Zero time, but maximum uncertainty
	•	Causes intro fatigue over time

⸻

4) CRM Notes (Only for Sales Teams)

How it works:
Log the intro in HubSpot/Salesforce/notes.

Why it fails:
	•	Too heavy for normal networking
	•	Doesn’t include the other people’s behavior
	•	Not cross-channel / not shareable

Cost:
	•	2–6 minutes per intro
	•	High abandonment outside sales orgs

⸻

5) Spreadsheets / Notion “Intro Tracker”

How it works:
Manual table of intros + follow-ups.

Why it fails:
	•	Manual updates die fast
	•	People forget to log outcomes
	•	Doesn’t solve the social awkwardness

Completion reality:
	•	Most users drop within 2–3 weeks
(because networking is chaotic)

⸻

6) “Warm Handshake” Over-Intro (Too Much Context)

How it works:
Introducer writes a long detailed intro hoping it reduces ghosting.

Why it fails:
	•	Doesn’t guarantee anyone replies
	•	Still no closure visibility later
	•	Takes too much time (burnout)

Cost:
	•	10–30 minutes per intro
	•	Scales badly

⸻

7) Group Chats / Threads (Slack/WhatsApp)

How it works:
Introducer makes a group chat so everyone stays visible.

Why it fails:
	•	People leave / mute / ignore
	•	Outcomes still not captured cleanly
	•	Not searchable or “proof-grade”

Cost:
	•	Medium effort, still unreliable

⸻

✅ ROOT CAUSES (WHY ALL WORKAROUNDS FAIL)

Root Cause #1: No shared “source of truth”

There is no universal object that represents:
	•	“this intro happened”
	•	“this is the status”
	•	“this is the outcome”

Root Cause #2: Manual follow-up creates social pain

People avoid awkward follow-ups → outcomes stay invisible.

Root Cause #3: Channels are fragmented

Email + LinkedIn + WhatsApp = broken continuity.

Root Cause #4: Outcome updates are optional

Nobody feels obligated to report outcome back to introducer.

Root Cause #5: No proof

Even when it works, introducer can’t prove performance later.
