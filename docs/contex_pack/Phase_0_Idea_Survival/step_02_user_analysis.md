TARGET USERS (4 CORE SEGMENTS — LOCKED)

Truferral is built for high-frequency introducers across four segments:

A) Recruiters (PRIMARY BEACHHEAD — LAUNCH FIRST)
	•	Who: Senior recruiter at a mid-size staffing firm (50–200 employees) in US metro markets
	•	Intros/month: 10–30
	•	Typical outcomes: Call booked → Interview → Offer → Hire / Rejection
	•	Why ideal: Clear outcomes, high volume, fastest feedback loop, strong ROI

B) Founders (SECONDARY)
	•	Who: Founder/CEO making partner, investor, hiring, or advisor introductions
	•	Intros/month: 3–15
	•	Typical outcomes: Meeting → Partnership → Investment → Hire / No-go
	•	Why strong: Reputation-sensitive, high leverage, time constrained

C) Sales / BD (SECONDARY)
	•	Who: Revenue teams using warm intros to close deals faster
	•	Intros/month: 5–25
	•	Typical outcomes: Intro → Meeting → Pipeline → Closed/Won
	•	Why strong: Direct revenue linkage = willingness to pay

D) SMB Owners (SECONDARY)
	•	Who: Local business owners relying on referrals and community trust
	•	Intros/month: 2–10
	•	Typical outcomes: Referral → Call → Booked Job → Repeat Customer
	•	Why strong: Community-driven retention and repeat value

⸻

BEACHHEAD (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

✅ Recruiters = Beachhead

Reason: Recruiters have the best combination of:
	•	highest intro volume
	•	fastest measurable outcomes
	•	strongest follow-up pain
	•	strongest “must track” behavior (pipeline mindset)
	•	highest willingness to pay for time + placement ROI

Rule: We do NOT build four different products.
We build one wedge that works for all segments, validated with recruiters first.

⸻

CORE PAIN (TRUFERRAL-ALIGNED — UNIVERSAL)

Across all four segments, the pain is the same:

Introductions happen across fragmented channels (LinkedIn/email/WhatsApp/events), but outcomes often disappear—so follow-through breaks and the introducer loses time, trust, and opportunity.

⸻

FREQUENCY + COST (REALISTIC RANGES)
	•	Frequency: weekly for all core segments
	•	Time cost: ~30–120 minutes/week in manual follow-ups and re-sends
	•	Opportunity cost: missed hires, missed deals, lost referrals
	•	Reputation cost: introducer looks unreliable without visibility
	•	Behavior outcome: users reduce intros over time (“not worth the hassle”)

⸻

CURRENT WORKAROUNDS (AND WHY THEY FAIL)
	1.	Email follow-ups

	•	threads get buried, no shared closure

	2.	Manual tracking (Notion/Sheets/Airtable)

	•	too much friction, inconsistent, abandoned under workload spikes

	3.	ATS/CRM notes

	•	not designed for cross-channel intro closure (especially across 2+ parties)

	4.	“Just ping them again”

	•	awkward, unreliable, not scalable

Conclusion: workarounds exist but do not create low-friction, consistent closure.

⸻

TRUFERRAL ENTRY POINT (HOW WE PULL TRAFFIC)

✅ Single entry action: “Create Intro Receipt link”

Why this works:
	•	People will keep discovering contacts on LinkedIn/Alignable/etc.
	•	But the intro itself runs through Truferral via a shareable link
	•	That link creates the habit loop: intro → status → closure

⸻

GO-TO-MARKET SEQUENCING (NO-PIVOT PATH)

Month 1–2: Recruiters only

Goal: prove wedge + retention
	•	100–300 recruiter users
	•	outcome closure improves vs baseline behavior

Month 3–4: Expand to founders + sales/BD

Goal: prove cross-domain fit
	•	500–1,500 total users

Month 5–6: Expand to SMB owners + circles/pods

Goal: retention flywheel + referral-driven growth
	•	2,000–5,000 users
