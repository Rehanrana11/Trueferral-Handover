PROBLEM STATEMENT (ONE SENTENCE — LOCKED)

“Professionals lose trust, time, and opportunity because introductions happen across fragmented channels, but outcomes often disappear—leaving no reliable closure or follow-through.”

⸻

	•	✅ Real, recurring user pain
	•	✅ No solution or product language
	•	✅ No platform names
	•	✅ Already happening today
	•	✅ Specific and falsifiable (“intros happen, outcomes disappear”)

⸻

VALIDATION: WHY THIS STATEMENT IS CORRECT

Who feels this pain most (high-frequency)
	•	Recruiters making candidate introductions
	•	Founders making partner or investor introductions
	•	Sales and business-development professionals routing referrals
	•	Community connectors introducing members

What breaks in the real world
	•	Introductions happen in DMs, emails, group chats, and events
	•	Intent to follow up exists, but priorities shift
	•	The introducer is left asking:
	•	“Did you two connect?”
	•	“Was this helpful?”
	•	“Should I re-intro?”
	•	Follow-ups become socially awkward, repetitive, and time-consuming

Emotional impact (what drives behavior)
	•	Frustration: “I don’t want to chase this again”
	•	Uncertainty: “I don’t know what happened”
	•	Reputation risk: “If this went nowhere, it reflects on me”
	•	Avoidance: “I’ll stop making intros altogether”

⸻

SURVIVAL ANALYSIS: WHY THIS PROBLEM WORSENS IN 2025–2026

AI increases volume, not certainty
	•	Outreach and “quick asks” are easier to generate
	•	Introduction volume increases
	•	Human follow-through remains the bottleneck
	•	More volume → more dropped threads → more invisible outcomes

Fragmentation is structural

Professionals do not network in one place:
	•	LinkedIn for discovery
	•	Email for serious introductions
	•	WhatsApp / iMessage for fast coordination
	•	Events for chemistry
	•	Slack communities for referrals

Workarounds exist but do not scale
	•	Spreadsheets, Notion, CRMs, and notes help some users
	•	They are manual, inconsistent, and rarely shared
	•	Most introductions still end in silence unless someone pushes

⸻

PLATFORM FAILURE ANALYSIS (WHY THE GAP PERSISTS)
	•	Discovery platforms are good at finding people, weak at closing outcomes
	•	Community platforms create engagement, not structured follow-through
	•	Matching platforms initiate connections but inconsistently capture results
	•	Referral platforms exist, but networking still occurs across external channels

Council-safe claim:
No incumbent provides seamless, cross-channel outcome closure at scale for everyday professional introductions.

⸻

WHY THIS IS THE RIGHT WEDGE FOR TRUFERRAL

This problem is:
	•	✅ Frequent for high-value networkers (weekly or more)
	•	✅ Measurable (intro created → outcome state)
	•	✅ Cross-platform by nature
	•	✅ Low-friction to test (a link can travel anywhere)
	•	✅ Retention-driving (users return for closure)
	•	✅ Growth-capable (sharing an intro naturally invites others)

⸻

OBSERVABLE USER BEHAVIORS (REAL-WORLD PROXIES)

Common phrases and behaviors:
	1.	“Just checking if you two connected…”
	2.	“Looping back on this intro…”
	3.	“Did anything come of it?”
	4.	Introducers mentally tracking outcomes, then giving up
	5.	People stopping intros because it feels risky and unrewarding

Conclusion: Introductions are common; outcome closure is unreliable.
